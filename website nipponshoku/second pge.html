Japan has the highest life expectancy in the world after Hong Kong. Japan’s current estimated life expectancy in 2021 is 84.79. This is their estimated longevity according to the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development. But what is the reason for their high life expectancy and how do they achieve it? The main and foremost reason behind their successful life expectancy rate is that Japanese people have a strict nutritious diet.The National Center for Global Health and Medicine in Tokyo recorded the diet habits of nearly 80,000 people over the course of 15 years. Their studies and results showed that the population follows dietary regulations or guidelines that have been given by the government since 2005. These guidelines provide how much of what food should be eaten in a day.

The basic guidelines are :

  • 5-7 servings of grain-based foods (rice, pasta, noodles, bread)
  • 5-6 servings of vegetables
  • 3-5 servings of meat and fish
  • 2 servings of fruit
  • 2 servings of milk and dairy products
  • Snacks, sweets, any junk food, and drinking alcohol are advised in moderation.

    • The study concluded that balanced consumption of energy, grains, vegetables, fruits, meat, fish, eggs, soy products, dairy products, confectionaries, and alcoholic beverages contribute to longevity by decreasing the risk of death, predominantly from cardiovascular disease, in the Japanese population. We can also see that with staple foods like omega-rich fish, rice, whole grains, tofu, soy, miso, seaweed and vegetables their diet is an ideal combination.
